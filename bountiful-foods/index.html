We Care Because We Live Here

The California South Coast region extends from Ventura County to the Mexican Border and includes the counties of Los Angeles, Orange and San Diego. It is known for its sandy beaches, surf-able waves, moderate climate, and large and diverse population. The beaches of the south coast are world famous recreational destinations that attract millions of visitors annually and are a major contributor to both the economy and the quality of life in the region. Southern California beaches typically receive over a million visitors each year. To us here at Bountiful Foods, the region is our home. That's why we've partnered with The Coastal Conservancy of California to give back to the places that shaped us. Ten percent of all proceeds go to support the conservancy's work.


Some Places We Love

South Ponto State Beach, Carlsbad, CA

South Ponto Beach is a rare gem in that it is one of the widest beaches in the county and exists in an undeveloped area. Coast Hwy 101 hugs the shoreline here, with Batiquitos Lagoon on the other side of the highway. The beach is a popular family destination, and only one of the few beaches in the area that has public restrooms and showers. If you happen to be driving on Coast Hwy 101 by this beach during sunset, you might find it irresistible to park on the highway shoulder and walk onto the sand to enjoy the captivating colors, wide stretch of sand, and water pounding on the jetty.

Oceanside Harbor Beach, Oceanside, CA

Bounded by the Oceanside Harbor and Cape Cod style Village on one side, and the Camp Pendleton Marine base on the other, this long, sandy beach is a perfect place to get away. Oceanside Harbor Beach is a wide sandy beach in front of Oceanside’s harbor. The beach is boxed in by two rock jetties at the ends which seems to hold in the large amounts of sand here. It’s a popular beach for sunbathing. Oceanside Pier is in the distance to the south and the Oceanside Marina is visible right behind the beach The sidewalks behind the beach and around the harbor offer miles of walking opportunities. The harbor has shops and restaurants if you plan to spend the entire day at the beach. You’ll also find volleyball courts, picnic areas, barbeques for grilling, and other beach amenities.

T-Street Beach, San Clemente, CA

One of the best surfing spots in Orange County, T-Street is the quintessential "Endless Summer" California beach hangout offering everything you need to enjoy a day in the sun. T Street Beach is found at the west end of Esplanade Street in San Clemente and that is why it is sometimes referred to as Esplanade Beach. T-Street is part of the San Clemente City Beach system. It has a safe pedestrian overpass over the railroad tracks from the parking areas at the “T” intersection of Esplanade and Paseo De Cristobal. The city maintains facilities at this palm tree-lined beach and provides lifeguards when it’s busy. The San Clemente Pier is just north and frequently photographed from this spot. The San Clemente Coastal Trail passes by here making it easy to walk to the pier or south to other beaches.


Main Beach Park, Laguna Beach, CA

Main Beach is the largest and most popular stretch of beach in Laguna. It is located in the middle of the town where Broadway and Ocean Avenues intersect Coast Highway. Among all the stretches of sand in Laguna Beach, none is more iconic, more recognizable, or more accessible to the masses than Main Beach. This is one of America’s truly “must visit” beaches. It’s bucket list worthy -- a place you really ought to experience for yourself. This is one of the most well-known beaches on the planet. Unless you’re extremely crowd averse, you should definitely see it at least once. Since it is in the middle of the town, it is a short walk from the beach to the numerous restaurant and shopping areas that are downtown.

Venice Beach, Los Angeles, CA

Venice Beach, which receives millions of visitors a year, has been labeled as "a cultural hub known for its eccentricities" as well as a "global tourist destination". The central hub of Venice Beach is at an area known as Muscle Beach, a nickname earned for the outdoor workout facilities and muscle display platform. Abutting Santa Monica’s south side on the Pacific Coast Highway, Venice Beach—or simply Venice—was developed as a Los Angeles beach resort that paid homage to its Italian namesake with canals, piazzas, pedestrian bridges, a lagoon, and a colonnaded business district. Designed by eccentric millionaire Abbot Kinney in 1905, Venice’s original waterways and charming beach cottages can still be seen on Dell Avenue in the Venice Canal Historic District.

South Beach, Del Mar, CA

Though it can be a bit confusing to differentiate between the Del Mar beaches, they are easy to find and walk between each one. South Beach is defined as south of Powerhouse Park all the way down to 6th Street at the southern end of Torrey Pines State Beach. This section is less popular for swimming and surfing than the neighboring Northern portion, but the high, jagged cliffs above it make for a beautiful and scenic beach walk.
